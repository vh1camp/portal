⚠️ Critical — Kidney Transplant & Medication Interactions

Humberto, please read this before starting any compound. You are on a serious immunosuppressant regimen (Tacrolimus + Mycophenolate) plus Carvedilol for cardiovascular support. These medications have narrow therapeutic windows and interact with several compounds in this protocol.

Required before starting: Share this protocol with your nephrologist and cardiologist. Request bloodwork to establish baseline tacrolimus trough levels, kidney function (creatinine, eGFR), and a cardiovascular baseline. Compounds flagged with ⚠ in this protocol require extra physician oversight — do not skip this step.

Key concern: Tacrolimus is nephrotoxic at elevated levels. Any compound that affects metabolism, hormone output, or fluid balance has the potential to shift tacrolimus blood levels. Monitoring trough levels during weeks 2 and 6 of this cycle is strongly recommended.

📌 First-Time Injector — Start Here

You noted you've never injected before — that's completely fine. Every compound in this protocol uses subcutaneous (SubQ) injections — shallow injections just under the skin, not into muscle. Use a 29–31 gauge insulin syringe, pinch skin on your abdomen or outer thigh, insert at a 45° angle, inject slowly. Nearly painless when done correctly. Rotate sites daily. You'll be comfortable with the process by week 2.

Storage & Handling

Powder vials (before reconstitution)

Refrigerate all lyophilized vials at 36–46°F (2–8°C). Keep from light and moisture. Do not freeze. Shelf life 12–24 months unopened.

After reconstitution

Refrigerate immediately. Use within 30 days (HGH within 21 days). Never freeze. Fresh syringe for every draw — critical with immunosuppression.

Bacteriostatic water

Use BAC water for all compounds — the benzyl alcohol preservative is essential for multi-draw vials. Order at least 6 BAC vials for this 9-compound stack.

Injection hygiene — extra important for transplant patients

Always wipe vial stopper and injection site with alcohol prep pad. Let air dry 10 seconds. Never reuse syringes. As an immunosuppressed patient, sterile technique is your single most important habit.
